Good, Better, Best Prompting Framework

The quality of your AI output depends on your input. Use the Good/Better/Best format to refine prompts.

Good Prompt

"Write an email about a new listing."

➡️ Result: Generic, vague, no personality or purpose.

Better Prompt

"Write a warm, personalized email to a luxury client in Washington State, highlighting a new $2.5M listing in Bellevue with a view of Lake Washington, focusing on privacy and outdoor living."

➡️ Result: More specific, but still basic.

Best Prompt

"Write a 150-word personalized email to a client who owns a penthouse in downtown Seattle and enjoys wine, art, and lake views. Introduce a new $2.5M gated home in Bellevue with a custom wine cellar, lakefront patio, and panoramic views. Use an elegant, conversational tone. Include one sentence about how this property complements their current lifestyle. End with a call to action to schedule a private viewing."

➡️ Result: Highly targeted, lifestyle-focused, emotionally engaging — perfect for high-net-worth clients.

Why it works: The best prompt adds context, audience, purpose, tone, and structure. This is AI’s GPS.

Prompting Pro Tips

  • Be specific about audience: “For a client who owns a penthouse in downtown Seattle” vs. “for a client”
  • Define tone: “Elegant, conversational, warm” vs. “neutral”
  • Include structure: “Write 3 paragraphs,” “Include bullet points,” “Use a question for engagement”
  • Specify length: “150 words,” “One sentence,” “A 60-second script”
  • Ask for options: “Give me 3 different angles for this email”

AI Use Cases for Seattle Luxury Agents

Here’s how you can use AI to serve high-net-worth clients — beyond just emails and posts.

1. Custom Market Reports

Use AI to generate personalized reports for clients, showing price trends, inventory, and comps in their target neighborhoods — all formatted professionally in minutes.

2. Design & Renovation Simulation

AI can generate design mockups for remodels (e.g., “Simulate what a modern kitchen renovation would look like in this 1920s home with marble countertops and a chef’s island”).

3. Client Persona Development

Input client data into AI: “Create a 100-word profile for a client who enjoys yoga, fine dining, and art — what kind of home would suit them in Seattle?”

4. Open House Ideas

AI can generate themes, decorations, music, and guest list suggestions for luxury open houses — tailored to the home and target audience (e.g., “Yacht club members,” “Art collectors”).

5. Property Storytelling

Turn a listing into a narrative: “Write a short story about the history of this 1913 Tudor in Magnolia — include its past owners, architectural details, and why it’s perfect for a creative family.”

AI Glossary (30 Terms)

1. AI (Artificial Intelligence): Machines that simulate human intelligence for tasks like writing, analysis, and decision-making.

2. Large Language Model (LLM): A type of AI that generates text, like ChatGPT or Gemini.

3. Prompt: The input you give to AI to generate a response.

4. Token: A unit of text (like a word or part of a word) used by AI for processing.

5. Context Window: The amount of text the AI can “remember” when generating output (e.g., 32,000 tokens).

6. Hallucination: When AI creates false information — always verify facts.

7. Fine-Tuning: Training an AI model to be better at a specific task (e.g., luxury real estate).

8. Natural Language Processing (NLP): How AI understands and generates human language.

9. Generative AI: AI that creates new content (text, images, audio, video).

10. Prompt Engineering: The skill of writing effective prompts for AI.

11. Embedding: A numerical representation of text used by AI to understand meaning.

12. Neural Network: A computing system inspired by the human brain, used in AI.

13. Latency: The time it takes for AI to respond — lower is better.

14. API (Application Programming Interface): A tool that lets software programs communicate.

15. Bias: When AI reflects unfair or inaccurate patterns from training data.

16. Agent: An AI that can take actions based on goals (e.g., “Find homes under $2M in Seattle”)

17. Persona: A role you assign to AI (e.g., “You are a luxury real estate agent in Seattle.”)

18. RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): AI that pulls real data before generating a response.

19. Chain-of-Thought: Prompting AI to show reasoning steps for complex tasks.

20. Zero-Shot: AI doing a task without prior examples (e.g., “Write a story about a Seattle home”)

21. Few-Shot: Giving AI examples before asking it to perform a task.

22. Fine-Tuning Dataset: Data used to train an AI model on a specific task.

23. Prompt Injection: A security risk where bad actors trick AI into doing harmful things.

24. Multi-Modal AI: AI that processes text, images, audio, and video together.

25. Tokenization: Converting text into tokens for AI to process.

26. Context: Background information given to AI to improve responses.

27. Output Length: The number of tokens or words AI generates.

28. Model: The AI system itself (e.g., GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini 1.5)

29. Temperature: Controls creativity — high = more creative, low = more predictable.

30. Prompt Library: A collection of saved, effective prompts for reuse.
