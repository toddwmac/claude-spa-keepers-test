üöÄ Advanced Prompt Engineering

The RACE Framework

Create better prompts using this proven structure:

  • Role: Define who the AI should act as
  • Action: Specify what you want done
  • Context: Provide relevant details
  • Expectation: Define format and tone
RACE Example:

Role: "Act as a luxury real estate marketing specialist familiar with Seattle's market."

Action: "Write an email to a potential buyer."

Context: "The buyer is a tech executive relocating from San Francisco, interested in Medina waterfront properties $4-6M with modern smart home features."

Expectation: "Professional but warm tone, 200 words, include 2 property recommendations and a meeting request."

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Don't: Copy AI Output Directly

Why: AI can make factual errors and lacks your personal expertise.

Do Instead: Review, fact-check, and personalize all AI-generated content before using it.

‚ùå Don't: Use AI for Legal Advice

Why: AI is not a licensed attorney and can miss critical legal nuances.

Do Instead: Use AI for preliminary research, then consult legal professionals for official guidance.

‚ùå Don't: Share Confidential Client Information

Why: Privacy violations can damage relationships and violate regulations.

Do Instead: Use generic examples or anonymized data when using AI tools.

‚ùå Don't: Rely on AI for Pricing Decisions

Why: AI lacks access to real-time MLS data and market intuition.

Do Instead: Use AI to organize comp data, but apply your professional judgment for final pricing.
