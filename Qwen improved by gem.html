2. The "Good, Better, Best" Framework

Effective prompting is the difference between a generic email and a bespoke client strategy. We use the Context-Action-Constraint model.

Good (The "Basic" Level)

"Write an email about a new luxury listing in Bellevue."

Why: Result is generic, lacks "flavor," and sounds like a robot wrote it.

Better (The "Specific" Level)

"Write a professional email to a high-net-worth client about a $5M modern listing in West Bellevue with Lake Washington views. Mention the privacy and the 4-car garage."

Why: Better context, but still feels like a template.

Best (The "Applied AI Labs" Level)

"Act as a luxury real estate concierge in Seattle. Write a confidential, low-pressure email to a C-suite executive client who values architecture and car collecting. Introduce an off-market $5M modern estate in West Bellevue. Highlight the 'gallery-style' garage and the sunset views over the Olympics. Tone: Sophisticated, brief, and exclusive. End with a suggestion for a private twilight tour."

Why: You've provided a Role (Concierge), a Persona (C-Suite), specific Emotional Triggers (Car collecting/Privacy), and a Tone (Sophisticated).
